# AGENT-007 Unreal-Style 3D Creative Suite Brief

Purpose:
- Give AGENT-007 and the GOAT crew a local-first 3D production workflow inspired by professional real-time engine practices.
- Support scene planning, asset tracking, camera blocking, character/voice direction, animation notes, render checklists, and Thor/GPU migration.
- Avoid copying proprietary Epic, Unreal, Daz, MetaHuman, Avid, Apple, Microsoft, Meta, TikTok, Spotify, or third-party code/assets.

Core workflow:
1. Scene bible: project title, story purpose, environment, tone, references, lighting style, target platform, and delivery format.
2. Asset registry: characters, props, environments, materials, textures, audio, music, licenses, creator/source, approval state, and storage path.
3. Shot board: shot number, camera move, lens feel, action, dialogue, emotion, VFX/animation notes, audio cues, and intended export.
4. Blueprint-style logic plan: player/viewer triggers, UI events, character states, animation transitions, dialogue routing, and data inputs.
5. Voice and performance: consent-approved voice profile, delivery style, accent notes, script, alternate takes, STT/TTS engine, and rights notes.
6. Render checklist: resolution, frame rate, codec, color pipeline, audio format, subtitles/captions, watermark, metadata, and delivery destination.
7. Evidence log: file hashes, source files, exports, dates, owner approvals, chain-of-custody, and client handoff notes.

Thor/GPU migration notes:
- Keep this Mac lane lightweight: planning, metadata, scripts, reports, and small previews.
- Move heavy 3D renders, long video analysis, large model inference, batch captioning, and GPU pipelines to Thor/Threadripper once staged.
- Track every model, engine, plugin, and asset license before client delivery.

Safety rules:
- Use professional workflow patterns, not cloned proprietary implementation.
- Do not expose private vault phrases, keys, passwords, private routes, payment settings, or client secrets.

- Do not claim a render, asset, fingerprint, or match exists unless the file/tool output confirms it.
